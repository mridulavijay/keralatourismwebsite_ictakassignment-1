Kollam

Kollam, known by its former name Quilon and Desinganadu, is an old seaport and city on the Laccadive Sea coast of the Indian state of Kerala.It is situated 66 kilometers north of the state capital Trivandrum.The city is on the banks of Ashtamudi Lake. Kollam is one of the least polluted cities in India.Kollam City boasts of a long history of political, commercial and cultural importance having found mention in several ancient travellers’ accounts. With a varied colonial past under the Portuguese, the Dutch and the British, Kollam has locations of historical importance like the Thangasseri light house, the St. Thomas Fort and cemetery built by the Portuguese, Thevally Palace, Cheenakkottaram etc. Besides, it also is the site of several old buildings constructed by the Travancore kings (its rulers during the pre-Independence times) in a variety of architectural styles, where many government offices still function. Four major trading centers around Kollam are Kottarakara, Punalur, Paravur, and Karunagapally. Kollam is the 5th largest city in Kerala and 5th in terms of corporation area.It is known for cashew processing and coir manufacturing. Ashtamudi Lake is considered the southern gateway to the backwaters of Kerala and is a prominent tourist destination at Kollam.
